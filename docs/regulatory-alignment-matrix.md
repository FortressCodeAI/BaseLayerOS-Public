# BaseLayerOS Regulatory Alignment Matrix

BaseLayerOS is designed as a **regulatory‑native governance substrate**.  
This document maps the 14‑layer architecture to major regulatory frameworks across:

- **Canada**
- **United States**
- **European Union**
- **Global standards**

The goal is to show how BaseLayerOS provides **structural compliance** rather than bolt‑on policy enforcement.

---

# 1. Canadian Regulatory Alignment

Canada has one of the most complex multi‑layered regulatory environments in the world.  
BaseLayerOS aligns with:

- **PIPEDA** — Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act
- **PHIPA (Ontario)** — Personal Health Information Protection Act
- **HIA (Alberta)** — Health Information Act
- **FOIPPA (BC)** — Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act
- **Quebec Law 25** — Modernized privacy law
- **OSFI B‑10 / B‑13 / E‑21** — Financial sector governance & technology risk
- **AIDA** — Artificial Intelligence and Data Act (upcoming)
- **DADM** — Directive on Automated Decision‑Making
- **Privacy Act** — Federal institutions

### Mapping:

| BaseLayerOS Layer | Canadian Regulation Alignment                              |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| CEL               | PIPEDA accountability, AIDA governance, DADM impact levels |
| IBEL              | PHIPA access control, OSFI identity governance             |
| DMAR              | AIDA risk mitigation, DADM transparency                    |
| ITFS              | PHIPA audit logs, OSFI B‑13 logging, Privacy Act evidence  |
| RIE               | AIDA → executable predicates, DADM → impact rules          |
| WCF               | PHIPA workflow safeguards, OSFI process controls           |
| GDAL              | PIPEDA purpose limitation, PHIPA minimum necessary         |
| SCGE              | AIDA risk assessment, DADM simulation requirements         |
| BSAE              | OSFI architecture documentation                            |
| CGIM              | OSFI governance roles                                      |
| AEP               | OSFI change management, AIDA continuous monitoring         |
| CIIPL             | Cross‑provincial identity harmonization                    |
| EDE               | DADM explainability                                        |
| NAS               | AIDA prohibited practices                                  |

# 5. Summary

BaseLayerOS provides **structural compliance** with:

- Canadian privacy, health, financial, and AI laws
- US sector regulations and NIST frameworks
- EU GDPR and AI Act

This is achieved through:

- constitutional enforcement
- identity‑bound execution
- deterministic multi‑agent runtime
- immutable forensics
- governed evolution
- universal integration SDK

BaseLayerOS is designed to be **compliance‑native**, not compliance‑adjacent.
